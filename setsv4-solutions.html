Sets Level 2

(1a) \(B-A\) is the set of all elements of \(B\) (\(\{40,60,80,100,120,\dots\}\)) that are not in \(A\). So filter out all the powers of 10 from \(B\), to get something like this: \[\{40,60,80,120,140,\cdots, 960, 980, 1020, 1040, \cdots\}\]

(1b) No, \(C\) is not equal to \(A\) because \(C\) contains negative integer powers of \(10\) (like \(0.1\) and \(0.0001\)) but \(A\) does not.

(1c) No, because of \(\{\emptyset\}\) were a subset of \(B\), it would mean that \(\emptyset\) is an element of \(B\), which it is not -- because \(B\) consists of numbers and \(\emptyset\) is not a number.
