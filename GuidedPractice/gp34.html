MTH 225 Guided Practice 3.4: Laws of Logic

Overview

We're pretty fluent at this point with logical propositions and how they can b combined, and how to use truth tables to know if two propositions are equivalent (although they may look different). What's next is to find some important equivalences that could be useful for us in reasoning through coding or mathematical problems. In this section we will discover a lengthy list of these laws of logic, by using truth tables to establish them.

Learning objectives

Basic objectives: Each student is responsible for gaining proficiency with each of these tasks prior to engaging in class discussions, through the use of the learning resources (below) and through the working of exercises (also below).

  • Use a truth table to prove one or more of the Laws of Logic in this section.

Advanced objectives: The following objectives are the subject of class discussion and further work; they should be mastered by each student during and following class discussions.

  • Apply one or more of the Laws of Logic to simplify or rewrite a logical expression.
