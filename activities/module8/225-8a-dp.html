Overview

Module 8 is the second of a three-module sequence on counting complex arrangements. In Module 8A, we look at two kinds of problems similar to but slightly different than the ones that gave us the binomial coefficient: Counting arrangements of things. We’ll look at how to count the number of ways to arrange nnn different objects, then how to arrange kkk elements chosen from a collection of nnn objects. In the process we will discover a closed formula for the binomial coefficient (nk){n}\choose{k}(kn​).

What you will learn

Learning Targets addressed in this module:

  • C.3 (Core): I can compute combinations and permutations and apply these to formulate and solve counting problems.

BEFORE your class meeting, use the Resources for Learning (below) to learn how to do the following:

  • Compute n!n!n! (nnn factorial) where nnn is a natural number.
  • Determine the number of permutations of a collection of nnn objects.
  • Determine the number of kkk-permutations of nnn objects.
  • State a closed formula for (nk){n}\choose{k}(kn​).

DURING AND AFTER your class meeting, you will learn how to do the following:

  • Solve counting problems that involve combinations and permutations.
